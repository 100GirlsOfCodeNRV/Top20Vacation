#20-Devil Towers,Wyoming's National Monument

The Devil Tower stands 5,112 feet above sea level is great for hiking on it's trial, the Devils Tower is a massive formation of laccolith rock in the Bear Lodge Mountains. It's been Wyoming's National Monument since September 24, 1906 when Presidnt Theodore Roosevelt declaired it one, The Devils Tower is a very popular spot to go and see in Wyoming with 400,000 annual visitors coming to climb.

#19-Lake Champlain, Vermont, Newyork, and Quebec
Lake Champlain is located in Vermon, New York, and Quebec crossing boarders with each state, the lake has a number of islands within it. The Lakes largest island holds many towns including Grand Isle and La Motte which are both located within the borders of Vermont. The lake is very spacious and is a beautiful place to visit, sunsets are spectacular and sitting on it's shore can be a very relaxing experince for all.

#18-Centennial Olympic Park,Georgia

The Centennial Olympic Park was the site of the 1996 Olympics and is now a popular park that millions of people come to visit in Atlanta, Georgia yearly to see the site of the famous Olympics. There are several events year round here in the park including a summer music concer and the annual Idependance Day concert and fireworks display. The park is surrounded by other Atlanta Landmarks including the Georgia Dome, the College Football Hall of Fame, and the Philips Arena.
